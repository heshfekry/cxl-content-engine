# Make it sound like you

This is the difference between generic content and content that is unmistakably yours. It
takes ten minutes and is worth more than any other tuning you can do.

## The principle: show, do not tell

Do not try to describe your own writing voice in a list of rules. Most people cannot, and
the rules never quite capture it. Instead, give the engine **examples** of your best writing
and let it work out the patterns itself. A language model is very good at reading a handful
of your articles and picking up how you actually write.

## How to do it

1. Pick **5 to 10 of your best articles**. The ones that sound most like you and did well.
   A mix helps: a how-to, an opinion piece, a deep dive.
2. Put them in `your-setup/voice-examples/` as `.md` or `.txt` files, or have links ready
   to paste.
3. Run `/setup`. When it reaches the voice step, it reads your examples and tells you what
   it found: how you open, your sentence rhythm, how you use evidence, your recurring moves.
4. **Correct it.** If something it inferred does not feel right, say so. This back-and-forth
   is the point. Often it will name a pattern you did not realise you had.

## Blend in an influence (optional)

You can also point it at a writer you admire and ask it to find a voice **between** yours and
theirs. For example: "Here are five of my posts and five from this writer I like. Analyse
both, then suggest a voice that keeps mine but borrows their structure." Then correct what it
proposes. You end up with instructions you never had to write yourself.

## Keep it fresh

As your writing evolves, swap in newer examples and run `/setup` again. The engine updates
what it knows about your voice. Your best recent work is always the best teacher.
