# How to steer an article (front-load your direction)

The single most effective thing you can do is give the engine a few sentences of direction
**before** it starts. A small steer at the start beats a lot of editing at the end.

You add the steer right after the keyword when you run the pipeline:

```
/blog-pipeline "content gap analysis" take a steal-your-competitors angle, feature our content gap tool heavily, and add a section on quick-win keywords competitors rank for that we do not
```

Anything after the quoted keyword is treated as your steer and shapes every step.

## What good steering covers

- **The angle:** "make this contrarian," "keep it beginner-friendly," "lead with a story."
- **Subtopics to include:** "make sure to cover X and Y."
- **Products to feature:** "mention our reporting feature in the measurement section."
- **What to avoid:** "do not turn this into a listicle," "skip the history lesson."
- **A piece to reference:** "bring in points from our article at this URL."

## You can also just talk to it

You do not have to cram everything into one line. You can run `/blog-pipeline "keyword"`
with no steer, look at the research output, and then tell the engine in plain English what
you want to change before it carries on. Both ways work.
