# Drop your best articles here

This is where the engine learns to sound like you. It matters more than any rule you write.

## What to add

Add **5 to 10 of your best articles**: the ones that sound most like you and performed
well. You can add them as:

- Markdown files (`.md`)
- Plain text files (`.txt`)
- Or paste the links during `/setup` and Claude will fetch them.

## What makes a good example

- It sounds unmistakably like you, not like a committee wrote it.
- It is the kind of article you want more of.
- A mix of formats helps: a how-to, an opinion piece, a deep dive.

## What happens next

When you run `/setup`, Claude reads these, works out the patterns that make them yours
(sentence rhythm, how you open, how you use evidence, your recurring moves), and writes
that summary into `brand-and-voice.md` for you to check and correct.

You do not need to describe your own voice. Let the examples do it. See
[docs/customise-your-voice.md](../../docs/customise-your-voice.md).

> Note: files you add here stay on your computer. They are ignored by git so you do not
> accidentally share them when you push the repo.
